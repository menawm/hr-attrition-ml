# HR Attrition Reduction Business Case

## 1. Executive Summary

Employee attrition is a preventable cost. This analysis demonstrates that a targeted, data-driven retention strategy can generate meaningful financial returns while reducing operational disruption.

Two intervention strategies were evaluated. The Recognition Program delivers the highest total savings and retains more employees, while the Career Development Program provides a higher return on investment due to lower costs.

The recommendation is to implement the Recognition Program as the primary strategy to maximize total business impact, supported by selective use of Career Development initiatives where cost efficiency is critical.

---

## 2. Business Problem

Attrition directly impacts the bottom line through replacement costs, lost productivity, and disruption to team performance.

Current retention approaches are typically reactive and broadly applied, resulting in:
- Missed opportunities to retain high-risk employees  
- Inefficient use of resources  
- Limited measurable impact  

The core challenge is not just reducing attrition, but doing so in a **targeted and financially efficient way**.

---

## 3. Approach

A predictive model was used to identify employees most likely to leave, enabling proactive intervention.

The approach:
- Analyze employee data to detect attrition risk  
- Use machine learning (KNN) to predict high-risk employees  
- Apply targeted intervention strategies  
- Evaluate financial outcomes based on retention impact  

This shifts retention from reactive action to **precision-based workforce management**. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## 4. Key Findings

### Recognition Program
- Highest total savings  
- Greatest number of employees retained  
- Higher upfront investment  

### Career Development Program
- Highest ROI  
- Lower cost per employee retained  
- Smaller total financial impact  

### Executive Insight
- Recognition maximizes **total value created**  
- Career Development maximizes **efficiency of spend**  

---

## 5. Financial Impact

Both strategies generate positive returns and justify investment.

- Recognition Program → higher total savings and stronger overall impact  
- Career Development Program → lower cost base and higher ROI  

**Implication:**  
The choice is not between “good” and “bad,” but between:
- maximizing total impact  
- maximizing efficiency  

---

## 6. Recommendation

**Implement the Recognition Program as the primary retention strategy.**

It should be prioritized because it:
- Delivers the greatest total financial return  
- Retains more employees, reducing operational risk  
- Drives broader organizational stability  

**Execution Approach:**
- Use Career Development selectively in budget-constrained scenarios  
- Layer both strategies where appropriate to balance impact and efficiency  

---

## 7. Implementation

- Deploy the predictive model to continuously identify at-risk employees  
- Target interventions rather than applying them broadly  
- Track retention outcomes and financial performance  
- Adjust strategy based on observed effectiveness  

This enables a scalable, data-driven retention system aligned with business outcomes.

---

## 8. Considerations

- Predictions are probabilistic and not perfectly accurate  
- Intervention success rates are assumed and should be validated  
- Results will vary based on execution quality  

Ongoing measurement and iteration will be critical to sustaining impact.
